New York City Transit Authority
Graphics Standards

by Unimark International / 1970

Why the change?

Prior to 1970 the New York City subway system was composed of three private companys, each one of them sporting different styles of signage and lettering from different designers.

In 1940 the company's were bought by the city of New York, forming them into the present day MTA in 1953.

The re-design of 1970 uniformed the signage from the private company's various rails they independently ran from one another.

Note from the designer

Please note that despite extensive research not all of the graphics are 100% true to the pre-1970 design change. The designer has used their best judgment in choosing the fonts for the pre-1970 train signs.
